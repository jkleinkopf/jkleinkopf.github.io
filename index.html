About

I am a science teacher at Salish Middle School in the North Thurston Public Schools district. I completed my M.Ed. and teacher certification at UMass-Boston through the Boston Teacher Residency. I previously completed my M.S. in plant sciences at Washington State University in the Roalson Lab and my B.A. at the University of Colorado.

Research Interests

As a biologist, I am interested in using phylogenetic tools to understand patterns of species distribution and to test hypotheses of species relationships. In particular, I am interested in how flowers are distributed and evolve on island systems. As an educator, I am interested in making science more accessible, and creating more opportunities for underrepresented groups of students to participate in and identify careers in the sciences. In the future, I intend to pursue a PhD to study the evolution and distribution of flower species while participating in science outreach.

Previously in the Roalson Lab at WSU, I used high-throughput sequencing and species-tree methods to explore the evolutionary histories of a subset of Hawaiian Cyrtandra species and to test for hybridization between species. Because these flowers are relatively young (~5.2 my), there has been much difficulty resolving relationships between these species. We found that our methods (hyb-seq and species-tree analyses) can work to resolve species relationships and test for hybridization within this group of plants. At CU, I worked in the Tripp Lab, using phylogenetic tools to test for evidence of speciation in the Purgatory Watershed of Southeastern Colorado and Northeastern New Mexico.
