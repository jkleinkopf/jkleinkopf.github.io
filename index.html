About

Joseph is a PhD student advised by Dr. Hannah Marx at The University of New Mexico and the Museum of Southwestern Biology Herbarium.

Research

How and why do some plants thrive in the harsh environments of the alpine, while others don't? When did alpine plants get here? I am interested in questions about the ecology and evolution of the alpine flora.

History

Previously, I taught 6th graders about earthquakes at Salish Middle School, studied the biogeography of a unique Hawaiian plant with Dr. Eric Roalson, and explored unique plants from the canyons of southeastern Colorado with Dr. Erin Tripp. View CV.

Papers

I have published works on Hawaiian Cyrtandra and morphologically anomalous plants of the Purgatoire Canyon. My publications can be found on my Google Scholar or Research Gate profiles.

Extra

I find meaning in becoming an effective teacher and using data to understand our world. In the future I hope to use data for the betterment of our schools and public spaces.
